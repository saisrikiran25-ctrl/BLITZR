BLITZR: Design & Appearance Specification
Internal Project Name: BLITZR-VISUAL-PRIME
Design Language: "Obsidian Industrial"
Target Aesthetic: Palantir Foundry meets Tesla Interface
1. Visual Identity & Design Language
1.1 The "Obsidian Industrial" Philosophy
BLITZR’s visual identity is built on three pillars: Authority, Density, and Precision. The interface should feel monolithic, utilizing high-contrast elements, sharp edges, and data-rich visualizations. It avoids rounded "bubbly" buttons in favor of slight corner radii (2px to 4px) and utilizes depth through subtle layering rather than drop shadows.
1.2 Color Palette (The Tactical Spectrum)
We move away from traditional "social" blues. The palette is strictly neutral with high-kinetic accents.
* Obsidian Base (#0A0A0B): The primary background color. A deep, true black that creates a sense of infinite depth on OLED screens.
* Titanium Gray (#1C1C1E): The secondary surface color. Used for cards, panels, and input fields to create structural hierarchy.
* Slate Neutral (#8E8E93): Used for secondary text and borders.
* Kinetic Green (#00FF41): The "Bull" color. A vivid, neon-tinted green used for gains, "Buy" actions, and active IPO statuses. Inspired by vintage terminal phosphors.
* Thermal Red (#FF3B30): The "Bear" color. A sharp, aggressive red used for losses, "Sell" actions, and the "Nuke" button.
* Active Gold (#FFD60A): Reserved exclusively for "Blue Chip" status, premium IPOs, and high-value Prop Events.
1.3 Typography: The "Authority" Grid
The typography must feel engineered.
* Primary Typeface: Inter or SF Pro Display. Used for headers and primary UI elements. It provides a clean, neutral, and professional appearance.
* Data Typeface: JetBrains Mono or Roboto Mono. Used for all numerical values, stock tickers, and prices. Monospaced fonts are mandatory to prevent "layout jumping" when prices fluctuate in real-time.
* Sizing: Strict adherence to a 4px grid. Smallest legible text (data labels) at 10px; Body at 14px; Headers at 18px-24px.
2. Global UI Components
2.1 The Glassmorphism Layering
Instead of traditional shadows, BLITZR uses "Material Backdrops." Panels have a 20% opacity white tint with a heavy (30px) background blur. This creates a "heads-up display" (HUD) effect, making the UI feel like it is floating over the data stream.
2.2 The Kinetic Ticker Tape
At the very top of every screen (except the Vault) is a 24px high marquee.
* Appearance: Monospaced white text on an Obsidian background.
* Behavior: Seamlessly scrolls real-time price changes of the top 10 most volatile IPOs and Chip-weighted Prop Events.
* Interaction: Tapping a ticker symbol instantly navigates the user to that entity's Dossier.
3. Tab-by-Tab Breakdown: The Interface Architecture
3.1 Tab 1: The Trading Floor (The Command Center)
The dashboard is designed for high data density. It is the "Bloomberg Terminal" for students.
* Header Section: A "Global Market Cap" counter that shows the total Creds in circulation, rendered in a large, glowing monospaced font.
* The Heatmap: A grid of 2x2 or 3x3 squares representing different campus hostel wings or departments. The squares glow green or red based on the aggregate movement of the IPOs within that category.
* Ticker Cards: * Layout: Horizontal cards with a "Sparkline" (a simplified, 24h line chart without axes) on the right side.
   * Visuals: The ticker name ($RAHUL) in bold white, current price in the Kinetic Green or Thermal Red, and a "Volume" bar at the bottom of the card showing activity.
* The "Pulse" Indicator: A subtle, 1px thick animated line at the bottom of the screen that "blips" whenever a trade is executed globally, providing a psychological sense of a living market.
3.2 Tab 2: The Arena (Prop Markets)
This tab is designed like a tactical mission board. Each event is a "Case."
* The Mission List: Vertical cards with high-contrast binary choices.
* The Sentiment Slider: Instead of just percentages, a dual-colored bar (Yes: Kinetic Green vs. No: Thermal Red) shows the current "Chip" weight. The slider is thick (8px) and rounded, filling up as more volume enters the market.
* The Odds Display: Displayed in a "Multiplier" format (e.g., 2.4x) to appeal to the high-stakes trading psychology.
* The Countdown Timer: A ticking clock in the top right of each card, rendered in a red monospaced font, indicating when the market settles.
3.3 Tab 3: The Rumor Feed (Intelligence Stream)
This is not a "Facebook Wall." It is a raw data feed.
* Micro-Post Design: Minimalist blocks of text. No profile pictures are shown (Anonymity Protocol). Instead, a "Ghost ID" icon (a geometric, procedurally generated shape) is used.
* The "Ticker Integration": Ticker symbols mentioned in posts are highlighted with a subtle glow. Tapping them reveals a "Mini-Terminal" (a bottom-sheet) with a 1-minute chart and quick "Buy/Sell" buttons.
* Data Overlays: If a post is causing a market move, a "Market Impact" badge appears, indicating the correlation (e.g., "$PRIYA -8% since this post").
* The Input Bar: Floating at the bottom, styled like a terminal command line. > Enter Intel...
3.4 Tab 4: The Dossier (Personal IPO / Profile)
When a user clicks a ticker, they enter the "Dossier." This is the Palantir-inspired entity view.
* The Profile Visualization: Instead of a circular avatar, a large, high-resolution portrait is used, potentially with a technical "scan-line" overlay.
* The Master Chart: A full-width, interactive TradingView-style chart. Users can toggle between "Line" and "Candlestick" views.
* Stats Grid: A 2x2 grid showing:
   * Market Cap: Total Cred value of all shares.
   * Liquidity: Number of Chips available for trade.
   * Holders: Total unique students owning this stock.
   * Dividend Yield: Total Creds earned by the owner.
* The Order Block: Two massive, industrial-strength buttons at the bottom: [ BUY ] in Kinetic Green and [ SELL ] in Thermal Red.
3.5 Tab 5: The Vault (Portfolio & Assets)
This is where the user manages their wealth. It should feel like a high-end banking app (like the Tesla app's energy or car stats).
* Net Worth Breakdown: A large donut chart centered on the screen. The outer ring is Creds; the inner ring is Chips.
* Asset List: A clean, searchable list of all active "Holdings." Each row shows the purchase price vs. current price with a clear "Profit/Loss" (P/L) indicator.
* The Transaction Ledger: A secondary view that looks like a printed receipt, showing every historical action with a timestamp.
4. Interaction Design & Motion
4.1 Haptic Feedback (Mechanical Precision)
BLITZR uses "Taptic" feedback to simulate physical machinery.
* The "Click": A sharp, medium-impact haptic when a trade is confirmed.
* The "Scroll": Light haptic ticks when scrolling through the Ticker Tape.
* The "Alert": A double-pulse haptic when a stock the user owns crashes by more than 10%.
4.2 Transitions & Animation
Motion is used to convey "live data," not decoration.
* Price Updates: When a price changes, the number doesn't just swap; it "rolls" like a digital odometer.
* Chart Wiggling: The line charts have a constant, tiny 1px jitter to indicate the "Market Maker" is active and the data is fresh.
* Screen Transitions: Screens slide horizontally with a "Motion Blur" effect (200ms duration) to feel fast and lightweight.
5. Specialized UI Features
5.1 The "Nuke" UI (The Emergency Fee)
When a user "Nukes" a market (freezing it), the entire screen briefly flashes a subtle red overlay, and a "Market Frozen" status bar appears with a countdown. The UI should feel heavy and consequential.
5.2 The "Delist" Procedure
This is a high-security action. When the "Delist My IPO" button is pressed, the user must hold the button for 3 seconds (a circular progress bar fills around the button). This prevents accidental exits and adds a sense of "Manual Override" to the action.
5.3 Dark Mode & Lighting
BLITZR is "Dark Mode First." There is no Light Mode.
* OLED Optimization: The use of pure black (#000000) for the background ensures the Kinetic Green and Thermal Red "pop" with incredible intensity, mimicking a dark cockpit or a mission control room.
6. Iconography: The "Industrial" Set
Icons are strictly 1.5px line-weight, non-filled, and geometric.
* Home: A stylized "Command" icon.
* Arena: A target or crosshair icon.
* Feed: A signal-wave or "Data Stream" icon.
* Vault: A geometric shield or vault-door icon.
* Dossier: A profile-outline within a bracket [ ].
7. Performance Requirements for Design
* Frame Rate: All animations and chart wiggles must maintain a steady 60fps.
* Latency: The "Buy" button must respond visually (button state change) within 16ms of a touch event, even if the backend transaction takes longer.
BLITZR represents the intersection of social dynamics and financial warfare. Every pixel is designed to reinforce the idea that information is the ultimate campus currency.
