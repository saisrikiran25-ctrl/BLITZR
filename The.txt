Layer 2: The Dean's Dashboard
Tell your coding agent to build a completely separate web portal (not part of the student app). Here are the exact instructions:
  

What to Build
A separate Next.js web app at admin.blitzr.app (or similar), protected by a unique login given only to the college's Student Affairs office or Dean.
Screen 1: Campus Sentiment Overview
* A line chart showing the average Clout Score movement across all listed students over the past 7 days
* A department-wise heatmap — same as the Trading Floor heatmap in the student app, but labeled "Dept. Sentiment Index"
* Zero individual names shown — everything is aggregated. The Dean sees "CS Dept sentiment is down 18%" not "$RAHUL is down 40%"
* Label this: "Campus Pulse Report"
Screen 2: Prop Market Predictions Panel
* A read-only list of all active Prop Markets on campus
* Shows the live Yes/No split as a percentage bar
* Example: "Will the Annual Fest be postponed? → Campus says 73% YES"
* Add a label: "Student Prediction Intelligence — Powered by BLITZR"
* This gives the administration genuinely useful data. Fest organizers can see student sentiment before making decisions
Screen 3: Content Safety Report
* A table of all posts that were auto-flagged by the moderation engine (from Layer 3 / Problem 3)
* Columns: Timestamp | Flagged Reason | Status (Reviewed/Pending) | Action Taken
* No post content is shown by default — the Dean clicks "Request Review" and a notification goes to your moderation team
* This gives the administration proof that you are actively moderating, without giving them direct surveillance power over students
Screen 4: Emergency Controls
* A single large button: "Pause All Markets" — freezes the entire campus economy for up to 24 hours
* A field to input a student's college email and manually trigger a Delist if the admin receives a complaint
* These controls exist so that if a Dean calls you, you can say "You have full override capability" — this makes them a partner, not an adversary
Database Requirements
Tell your coding agent:
"All data fed to the Dean's Dashboard must be read from aggregated, pre-computed views — never raw user tables. Create a scheduled job that runs every 15 minutes and writes summarized statistics to a separate admin_analytics table. The admin portal only has read access to admin_analytics. It cannot query users, transactions, or rumor_posts directly."
This is critical — it protects student privacy legally and prevents the admin portal from becoming a surveillance tool.
